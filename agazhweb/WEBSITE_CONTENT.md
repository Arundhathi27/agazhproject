---

#### Program 1 — Heritage Education Programs

🏛️ **Heritage Education Programs**

**Tags:** Year-Long Initiative · Grades 6–9 · In-Campus · Interdisciplinary

**Subtitle:** Experiential Heritage Learning (EHL) Programme

**What It Is:**
> The Experiential Heritage Learning (EHL) Programme is a year-long, in-campus educational initiative that integrates heritage, history, environment, and cultural knowledge into structured, activity-based modules. The program translates textbook-based learning into experiential modules and integrates academic learning, tourism, and social awareness to make archaeology engaging and accessible.

**Who It's For:**
> The program is primarily targeted at middle-school students in the 6th through 9th grades across all types of schools. Additionally, our broader educational initiatives are intended for schools, colleges, and lay audiences.

**Outcome & Impact:**
> Students reinforce their curricular concepts through field engagement, observation, and interdisciplinary learning. The program nurtures learners to explore and contribute meaningfully to archaeology, history, and society. Ultimately, it promotes critical thinking, ethical awareness, and a responsible engagement with local and regional heritage.

---

#### Program 2 — Workshops & Storytelling Sessions

🪄 **Workshops & Storytelling Sessions**

**Tags:** Interactive · Pottery-Making · Excavations · Heritage Trails

**Subtitle:** Hands-On Archaeology & Field Explorations

**What It Is:**
> We conduct interactive hands-on Archaeology Workshops featuring practical sessions, pottery-making, and simulated excavations. We also bridge classrooms with real-world experiences through field explorations and heritage trails.

**Who It's For:**
> These sessions are designed for students, schools, colleges, and the general lay audience.

**Outcome & Impact:**
> By combining fieldwork and practical learning, we inspire a genuine appreciation for the past. These programs foster curiosity, creativity, and a deeper, personal connection to living history and cultural legacies.

---

#### Program 3 — School Activities & Clubs

🎓 **School Activities & Clubs**

**Tags:** 4 Classes/Month · Career Guidance · Grades 6–9 · All School Types

**Subtitle:** Extra-Curricular Heritage Learning (EHL)

**What It Is:**
> Our Extra-Curricular Heritage Learning (EHL) integrates seamlessly into the academic schedule without interfering with prescribed assessments or syllabi, offering 4 classes per month. We also organize Career Guidance Programs for students interested in seeking opportunities within the humanities and heritage sectors.

**Who It's For:**
> These activities are tailored for students in the 6th to 9th age category across all types of schools.

**Outcome & Impact:**
> Students learn to apply classroom concepts through experiential, activity-based learning. They develop crucial skills in observation, inquiry, basic documentation, and collaborative communication. The activities are designed so students experience, relate, and think better, allowing them to apply this knowledge directly to their academics.

---

#### Program 4 — Events

🗺️ **Events**

**Tags:** Heritage Walks · Site Visits · Awareness Campaigns · Open to All

**Subtitle:** Heritage Walks, Explorations & Awareness Campaigns

**What It Is:**
> We lead heritage explorations, awareness campaigns, and specific site visits, such as Heritage Walks.

**Who It's For:**
> Events are open to schools, colleges, and the lay audience.

**Outcome & Impact:**
> These events help ensure that India's past remains protected, valued, and understood by future generations. By exploring unexplored sites and engaging in heritage tourism, participants build a personal connection to cultural legacies through direct discovery.
